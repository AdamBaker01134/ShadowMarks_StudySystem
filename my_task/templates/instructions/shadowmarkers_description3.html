Shadow Marks

A Shadow Mark is an object that can be placed in one display, and will be shadowed (or mirrored) in all other displays at the same location. An example Shadow Mark is the green marker visible in all of the videos of Figure 1.

Fig.1. Example of a Shadow Mark placed within a baseball pitching video and shadowed in all other videos.


In this example, the user places a Marker Shadow Mark at the pitcher's release point in the first video by clicking on the dispaly. The system duplicates the marker at the same location in all the other videos. Now the user can compare this position to all the other videos.

Shadow Mark colour can be customized by clicking the colour button (Figure 2 left) and selecting a colour from the colour palette (Figure 2 right).

Fig.2. Shadow Mark colour button (left) which opens a colour palette (right) for selection.


You can play through the videos by pressing SPACEBAR, or by dragging the scrollbar located at the bottom of the page (as seen in Figure 3). Watch frame by frame by tapping the LEFT ARROW or the RIGHT ARROW.

Fig.3. Scrollbar that controls all videos.


You zoom in by holding CONTROL and pressing the + key, and zoom out by holding CONTROL and pressing the - key.

If you forget any of the features available, click on the help button located in the bottom left corner (as seen in Figure 4).

Fig.4. Help button.
