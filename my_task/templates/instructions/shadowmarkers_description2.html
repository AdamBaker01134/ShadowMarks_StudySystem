Shadow Marks

A Shadow Mark is an object that can be placed in one display, and will be shadowed (or mirrored) in all other displays at the same location. An example Shadow Mark is the red cursor visible in all of the videos of Figure 1.

Fig.1. Example of a Cursor Shadow Mark placed within an Arctic ice video and shadowed in all other videos.


In this example, the user has placed a Cursor Shadow Mark at the rightmost edge of the ice. The system duplicates the lines at the same location in all the other videos. Now the user can compare the ice extent of all the videos by observing which videos extend to the right past the vertical line.

Shadow Mark colour can be customized by clicking the colour button (Figure 3 left) and selecting a colour from the colour palette (Figure 3 right).

Fig.3. Shadow Mark colour button (left) which opens a colour palette (right) for selection.


You can play through the videos by pressing SPACEBAR, or by dragging the scrollbar located at the bottom of the page (as seen in Figure 4). Watch frame by frame by tapping the LEFT ARROW or the RIGHT ARROW.

Fig.4. Scrollbar that controls all videos.


You can also zoom in by holding CONTROL and pressing the + key, and zoom out by holding CONTROL and pressing the - key.

If you forget any of the features available, click on the help button located on the left side of the scrollbar (as seen in Figure 5).

Fig.5. Help button.


Warning: If you switch tabs, exit fullscreen, or move your cursor out of the webpage for too long, you will redirected to another screen and you will need to restart the task.
