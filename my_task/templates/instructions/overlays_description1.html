Overlays

An Overlay is an arrangement of displays that are stacked on top of one another. The top layers are made to be translucent so that you can see through to the bottom layers. This allows you to compare objects within each display all in the same location. An example of an overlay can be seen in Figure 1.

Fig.1. Two sunflower videos overlaid within a single display.


The workspace you are working in includes an Overlay display to the right of the loaded videos, as seen in Figure 2.

Fig.2. Example of a Overlay layout of 9 plant videos.


The "eye" in the top right-hand corner of each video indicate whether or not it is visible within the Overlay. If the eye is closed (Figure 3 left), then the video is not in the Overlay. If the eye is open (Figure 3 right), then it is in the Overlay. The number displayed within the eye indicates the videos position in the Overlay, where 1 is the bottom layer.

Fig.3. Overlay presence indicator. Closed eye (left) indicates video is not within Overlay. Open eye (right) indicates video is within Overlay.


Clicking on a video will add it to the Overlay. Clicking on it again will remove it from the Overlay.

You can play through the Overlay as well as all the other videos by pressing SPACEBAR, or by dragging the scrollbar located at the bottom of the page. Watch frame by frame by tapping the LEFT ARROW or the RIGHT ARROW.

You can add/remove one video repeatedly to see differences. You can also zoom in by holding CONTROL and pressing the + key, and zoom out by holding CONTROL and pressing the - key.

If you forget any of the features available, click on the help button located on the left side of the scrollbar.
